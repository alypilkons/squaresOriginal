Basic Color Theory

There are three PRIMARY colors: blue, red, and yellow.

There are also three SECONDARY colors: orange, purple, and green.

Secondary colors are created by mixing two primary colors.

COMPLEMENTARY colors are two colors directly opposite each other on the color wheel. Each pair of complementary colors is made up of one primary color and one secondary color.

How To Play

After pressing START, two or more colored squares will appear in a line. Your job is to create your own line of squares in response to that line.

For example, you might see this line of squares:

And you would respond by creating this line of squares:

You do this by combining each square with the one to its right to create a new color square, following five simple rules. Hint: Use color theory!

Rule 1: Two matching colors combine into one copy of the color

Rule 2: Primary colors combine to create appropriate secondary color

Rule 3: Two secondary colors combine into the primary color both need to make their color. For example, purple is made from red and blue, and green is made from yellow and blue, so they both require blue.

Rule 4: A primary and a secondary color combine into the other primary color needed to create that secondary color. For example, to create orange, red needs to be mixed with yellow.

Rule 5: If there are any complementary colors in the line, skip them altogether and move onto the next pair of squares!

How To Play, continued

Remember that example from the last screen? How do you go from this...

...to this?

Let's work through it step by step. You start with the purple square on the left, and the orange square next to it. Purple and orange are both secondary colors, and they both need red to make them. So the first square in your response is red!

Next you move on to the orange square and the green one next to it. They're both secondary colors too! Which primary color do they both need to make their colors? Yellow! So you add yellow to your line.

Next up is green and blue. Green is secondary, but blue is a primary color. What other color needs to be mixed with blue to make green? Yellow! So another yellow square is added to the line.

The final two squares in the line are blue and red, both primary colors. Mixing them gives us purple, so the last square in our line is purple! And we're done!
